# grass

Vite + React 19 + React Three Fiber demo: 72k instanced grass blades on a 3x3 tile grid.

## Structure

- `src/App.jsx` тАФ Canvas, scene composition, ground plane, soil block. Scene switching is a two-target compositor, not a flip: `Scenes` mounts the grass diorama and `<City>` into two separate `THREE.Scene`s via `createPortal`, renders BOTH every frame into two `useFBO` targets (4x MSAA), and the default scene contains only a fullscreen `ScreenQuad` with `blendMaterial` compositing the two textures (`uMix`, smoothstepped `p` 0тЖТ1 over ~1.6s driven by the `.flip-btn` toggle) тАФ not a crossfade: an fbm-raggedized diagonal front sweeps the frame with heat-ripple UV warp and an additive ember rim at the dissolve edge, and the threshold overshoots by the edge width so both endpoints fully resolve. Both scenes stay mounted and animating at all times; StylePass grades the composed quad. Transitions also zoom in 3D: the grass and city portal contents are each wrapped in a scale-animated `group` (`grassRoot`/`cityRoot`, driven in the same `useFrame`) тАФ the incoming diorama dollies up 0.55тЖТ1 while the outgoing recedes 1тЖТ0.55, paired with MichiganHub's own map dive toward the clicked destination; the sky quads sit outside these roots so they stay fullscreen. Each portal scene also carries a billboarded troika title (drei `<Text>` in `SceneTitle`: "UP NORTH" / "DETROIT") hung below the diorama's front corner at [4.8,-3.4,4.8] (nameplate under the soil block's front tip) тАФ depth-test off so buildings never occlude it, and being in-scene it rides the burn wipe like everything else. Each scene renders its own sky тАФ the meadow uses the shared leva-driven `skyMaterial`, the city a module-level clone with the steely palette baked in тАФ so there's no palette-crossfade bookkeeping. All canvas content sits in one `<Suspense fallback={null}>` with the custom `<MittenLoader />` overlay (`src/MittenLoader.jsx`) outside: the GLB (mounted eagerly) suspends the whole scene until loaded, which is the preload. The loader is a Michigan-themed splash тАФ stylized Upper/Lower Peninsula SVG paths that fill bottom-up via a clip rect, "PURE MICHIGAN" label тАФ driven by drei `useProgress` with a 4s minimum: fill = min(real progress, elapsed/4s), and it only fades out (CSS opacity, then unmounts) once both assets are loaded and 4s have passed. The map hub is the default opening scene (`#city`/`#meadow` deep-link straight into a diorama); when the loader finishes it fires a `mitten-done` window event (and sets `window.__mittenDone`) that Camera.jsx and CloudCover.jsx use to start the load intro тАФ a quick camera push-in while the map's extra cloud blanket dissipates. Caveat: the serene style's depth-based haze reads depth from the quad scene (all 1.0), so it's inert under this architecture.
- `src/city/City.jsx` тАФ downtown Detroit diorama, authored ground-at-y=0, mounted at the origin of its own portal scene. The whole scene is one GLB (`public/detroit.glb`): Tripo-generated buildings (RenCen, stadium, arena, civic tower, brick block) plus baked ground-with-river, roads, sidewalks, park, and trees; Draco-compressed meshes, KTX2 textures. `BakedCity` loads it via drei `useGLTF` (no local Suspense тАФ it suspends up to App's loading screen; Draco via drei's default CDN decoder, KTX2 via a module-singleton `KTX2Loader` pointed at the drei-assets basis CDN and `detectSupport`ed against the renderer), strips the GLB's embedded cameras/light, and swaps every mesh to unlit `MeshBasicMaterial` (lighting is baked into the textures) тАФ except the `Detroit_River_Blue` primitive, which gets a module-singleton animated river `ShaderMaterial` тАФ deliberately calmer than `Ocean.jsx`: dark steely two-tone ripple drifting along the corner diagonal, no crests/sheen/glint/sparkles. It reuses the shared `uTime` uniform object from `grass/material.js`, so Grass's frame loop animates it (Grass is always mounted тАФ both scenes render every frame). The material swap in the traverse also re-points any prior ShaderMaterial (StrictMode double-run / HMR on the cached mutated scene) so the river never falls into the basic-material branch and goes white. Scene is authored 30├Ч30 with ground at y=0, so it mounts at scale 0.5 to fit the 15├Ч15 slab. No light rig; the only other pieces are the unlit slab box under the ground and `<People>` тАФ a module-singleton group of two InstancedMeshes (cylinder bodies with per-instance shirt colors + skin-toned sphere heads, unlit) scattered in hand-picked clumps around each landmark, authored in GLB coords and mounted at the same 0.5 scale; ~70% of them walk small tangent-facing circles around their spawn point with a step bob (per-person params baked at module load, matrices recomposed in a `useFrame`), the rest stand. `<PeopleMover>` is the Detroit People Mover: a module-singleton closed `CatmullRomCurve3` loop (authored coords, elevated to y=1.3) rendered as an unlit concrete `TubeGeometry` guideway on 36 instanced pillars, with a two-car train in DPM livery (white box body + half-cylinder rounded ends, dark upper window band, grey roof, teal accent stripe, dark bogie skirt riding the tube) whose `useFrame` slides both cars along the curve (~70s/lap) via `getPointAt`/`getTangentAt` + `lookAt` (the lookAt target is local+tangent scaled by the mount's uniform 0.5). The waypoints were verified against the GLB's building AABBs (extracted by parsing detroit.glb in node with a Worker/fetch-shimmed GLTFLoader) тАФ the sampled curve clears every building footprint by тЙе0.3 authored units; re-run that check if buildings move.
- `src/MichiganHub.jsx` тАФ the map hub scene: extruded UP + mitten geometry (built from `MittenLoader`'s SVG paths), water plane + grid, and two `DestinationMarker`s (Detroit / Up North) with mini previews, pulsing rings, floating pins, and labels. Labels are billboarded MANUALLY in the marker's `useFrame` (local quaternion = `billboardQuat` (inverse of the static tiltтИШy-angle parent rotation, computed in render) ├Ч camera quaternion) тАФ drei's `<Billboard>` useFrame never fires inside this FBO portal, so its labels rendered skewed. The water plane + gridHelper are 60├Ч60 (0.5 cells) so the checkered backdrop covers the whole window at any aspect. A "map" leva folder drives tilt (quaternion about the (1,0,тИТ1) `TILT_AXIS` diagonal toward the iso camera, on a group outside the animated `mapRoot` so the dive math is untouched), zoom (base scale multiplied into the dive scale in the `useFrame`), and angle (the land group's rotation-y, also used for the dive's focus rotation); each marker's pointer target is a single invisible cylinder (`colorWrite`/`depthWrite` off) sized to the visible marker (preview footprint through label) so hover/click is one solid volume, not per-mesh slivers. The land itself is NOT interactive тАФ markers (and the HUD buttons, synced via `hoverHubDestination`) are the only click targets; `mapRoot` scales/translates in a `useFrame` to dive toward the clicked destination during scene transitions (the tilt group co-rotates the offset, so at full focus the destination lands at the scene origin).
- `src/CloudCover.jsx` тАФ drifting low-poly cloud decks, one `CloudLayer` instanced mesh per portal scene (mounted inside each scene's portal in App.jsx, so clouds ride the burn wipe and never bleed across scenes тАФ the map's deck no longer sits in the default compositor scene). Clusters of 7 y-rotated icosahedron puffs wrap-drift across a configurable area in a priority-0.4 `useFrame` (before the FBO pass at 0.5). Unlit on purpose: the cel look is baked into per-face vertex colors (3-band ramp by face-normal height тАФ y-rotation preserves normal.y so the bake holds per instance) on two module-singleton geometries, `warm` (map + meadow) and `steel` (Detroit), sharing one `MeshBasicMaterial` тАФ no light rig, so nothing fights the map's bright rig or the city's deliberately unlit scene. `CloudCover` is the leva-tuned map deck ("cloud layer" folder; color sliders retint the warm geometry in place) тАФ it takes App's mutable `hubTransition` as a `transition` prop and runs its own intro clock off it (the wipe's `p` is too short): clusters bloom outward from their center point (puff offsets and scales both ride the intro factor) as a front-to-back spatial sweep (delay keyed to x+z screen depth under the iso camera, narrow smoothstep ramp) over ~3.4s whenever the map is the incoming/resident scene, and sweep out front-to-back too in ~1.2s (inside the 1.6s wipe; the delay axis flips with direction) when the map is outgoing. Load intro: `IntroStratus` тАФ a dense warm stratus sheet (module-singleton `makeCloudSheetMaterial`, cover 1.15 тАФ past the shader's solid point so it starts gapless; a 240├Ч110 plane, deeper than the diorama sheets since the intro camera holds a pulled-back zoom, with the same near edge but the extra depth pushed back along (тИТ1,0,тИТ1)/тИЪ2 to stay inside the ortho far box) blankets the map; when `window.__mittenDone` flips, its `uCover` burns down to 0 over ~1.4s (opacity chased out at the tail) matching Camera.jsx's quick push-in, then the mesh unmounts for good; the meadow/city clouds are `CloudSheet`s instead тАФ a single transparent horizontal plane per diorama (inside `grassRoot`/`cityRoot` so they scale with the transition dolly; 170├Ч65, spun 45┬░ about y so its width runs along the iso camera's screen-x and clears both screen edges at any aspect, pushed back so it fits the ortho near/far box тАФ the camera-side uv.y fade dissolves clouds before the near-clip line crossing the layer) whose fragment shader draws wispy stratus: fbm noise rotated into the wind frame and squashed across-wind into long streaks, coverage-thresholded to alpha with a soft rectangular edge fade and a shade tone mixed into denser cores; no volume, no lights, no useFrame тАФ the two module-singleton materials (warm / steel) ride the shared grass `uTime` like Ocean/river. Each sheet has its own leva folder ("stratus ┬╖ up north" / "stratus ┬╖ detroit"): all knobs write transiently to that tint's uniforms (cover/opacity/softness/streak scales/speed/direction/colors, initialized from the preset) except altitude, which re-renders the mesh position.
- `tools/blender/` тАФ the (now legacy) bake pipeline that generated the previous Blender-authored detroit.glb: drives Blender through the blender-mcp addon socket (`bmcp.py`), builds the scene in three.js coordinates (`P(x,y,z)тЖТ(x,-z,y)`, glTF export converts back), joins into 2 atlas objects, smart-UVs, Cycles-GPU-bakes COMBINED, exports the GLB. The current GLB is Tripo-sourced, not produced by this pipeline.
- `src/StyleEffect.jsx` тАФ the only post-processing: one `postprocessing` Effect with a `uMode` switch behind a "style" leva dropdown (none / ink / anime / outline / b&w) plus line-strength and color-band sliders. All modes share a 3├Ч3 sobel on alpha-weighted luma; `none` unmounts the EffectComposer entirely. Replaced the old Bloom + Kuwahara stack.
- `src/Camera.jsx` тАФ orthographic isometric corner view at [12.5,11,12.5] targeting (0,-1,-0.4): the square tile reads as a floating diamond diorama (target below ground keeps the soil block's underside in frame). Aspect-aware `view` half-height with clamps; OrbitControls constrained to a narrow polar/azimuth window around the corner angle. Intro dolly on load: while the MittenLoader runs the camera holds a pulled-back zoom (0.55x of resting, `minZoom` widened via `intro` state); on the loader's `mitten-done` event it pushes in to the composed view over ~1.1s тАФ zoom only, no azimuth/polar swing. Driven manually in a `useFrame` (easeOutCubic on a clamped-dt progress, applied via `zoomTo` with transitions off) тАФ NOT CameraControls' damped transitions, because the loader fade lands right after the city's mount/compile hitch and the multi-second frame delta would snap the transition to the end pose before reveal. `intro` flips off at p=1, restoring the tight interaction clamps. Takes App's `activeScene` as a `scene` prop: on any scene flip it eases the shared camera back to the composed default pose/zoom (same move as the drag-release restore), so a zoomed-in view never carries into the incoming scene. A very slight mouse-parallax yaw (`MOUSE_YAW`, damped in a `useFrame`) lives on a parent `group` around the camera тАФ CameraControls owns the camera's local pose, so the sway never fights it.
- `src/coast.js` тАФ single source of truth for the in-tile coastline: `COAST_EDGE` (grass is fully dune sand by z=-COAST_EDGE), `LAND_EDGE_Z` (ground plane discards past this; the ocean plane below shows through), `WATERLINE_Z`, plus the shared sand/dune-grass colors + uniforms that keep ground, grass, ocean, and soil block sand identical. The grass vertex shader, ground fragment, and SoilBlock all interpolate these constants.
- `src/grass/constants.js` тАФ GRID/TILE/blade counts/param-texture layout constants.
- `src/grass/shaders.js` тАФ GLSL: wind bend + hash-cell clump pull (uClump, cell size from uClumpScale, mound-shaped height taper toward each clump center) + param fetch in vertex, fbm color fields + gust sheen (vGust varying) + AO in fragment. Interpolates constants into the source.
- `src/grass/geometry.js` тАФ `buildGeometry()`: one normalized segmented strip as `InstancedBufferGeometry` with per-blade attributes (offset, scale, rotation, wind phase, tile id). The vertex shader shapes it with live blade-profile uniforms. Blades are laid out blade-major (tiles innermost) so the "blades" slider can truncate `instanceCount` and thin all tiles evenly.
- `src/grass/tileParams.js` тАФ the per-tile `DataTexture` (4 RGBA float texels per tile: colors A/B/C + scales/mixes) and `randomizeTiles()` which rewrites it with hue/sat/lightness-jittered colors. Jitter is seeded (mulberry32) so slider changes re-tint without reshuffling the layout; only `reseed()` (the "randomize" button) rolls a new arrangement.
- `src/grass/material.js` тАФ the `uniforms` object + ShaderMaterial, built manually as module singletons тАФ R3F's `uniforms` prop clones each uniform into the material, so mutating a shared uniforms object via JSX `<shaderMaterial>` never reaches the GPU. Exported so App.jsx's "ground / blade base" control can drive `uBaseColor` too (ground plane and blade roots share one color). Also exports `groundMaterial`: samples the density mask for pressed-turf/soil tint, embosses two-scale noise on the soil for path bumpiness, and reuses the shared `uTime`/`uGustScale`/`uSheen` uniform objects to run the same traveling gust-sheen formula as blades/rocks so the wash lightens the path in sync.
- `src/grass/densityMask.js` тАФ 256┬▓ CPU-baked `DataTexture` carving a meandering dirt path: R = surviving blade density, G = pressed-grass amount (also darkens the ground), B = exposed-soil patches. Sampled by both the grass vertex shader (kills/shortens blades) and `groundMaterial` (pressed-turf + soil tint). `setPathParam(key, value)` re-bakes the whole texture (width/soil/clearing/core), driven by the "path" leva folder.
- `src/grass/Grass.jsx` тАФ the mesh + leva controls (`useControls`); color/scale changes and the "randomize" button call `randomizeTiles`. A "wind" folder (strength/speed/gust scale) writes transiently to the `uWindStrength`/`uWindSpeed`/`uGustScale` uniforms via `onChange` тАФ no re-render. A "path" folder mixes both patterns: width/soil/clearing/core call `setPathParam` (CPU mask re-bake), press/soilColor/darken/bump/bumpScale write to uniforms. Path defaults live in `PATH_DEFAULTS` in `defaults.js`.
- `src/rocks/rockMask.js` тАФ 256┬▓ world-space occupancy `DataTexture` (`uRockMask` in the grass material); keeps two footprint lists тАФ `stampRockMask(footprints)` (re-stamped on every rock scatter) and `stampSceneryMask(footprints)` (static, from Scenery.jsx) тАФ merged into one bake so neither wipes the other. Writes soft-rimmed circles (solid kill under the rock, fading out to `RING`├Ч its radius) and the grass vertex shader scales blades down by the mask тАФ grass never renders through rocks and ramps up gradually around each base so rocks look nestled in.
- `src/Ocean.jsx` тАФ Ghibli-style beach + ocean INSIDE the tile along the -z edge (where the dirt path exits): one flat plane exactly as wide as the field, spanning from just inland of `LAND_EDGE_Z` to the tile edge, tucked below the ground plane (the ground discards past `LAND_EDGE_Z`, so the ocean shows through there). Everything painted in the fragment shader keyed off signed distance past a breathing waterline (run-up/retreat + wobble) тАФ mottled dry sand (world-space formula identical to the ground's dune sand, hiding the discard seam) тЖТ wet sand with a foam-arc "ghost" тЖТ scalloped foam + broken second line тЖТ gouache-banded turquoise-to-teal compressed into the short in-tile band. Vertex bob on open water only. Reuses the shared `uTime` uniform object from `grass/material.js`, so Grass's frame loop animates it тАФ no `useFrame` here. Module singleton, no controls.
- `src/SoilBlock.jsx` тАФ diorama base: a 15├Ч2├Ч15 box under the tile painted entirely in-shader тАФ mottled soil gradient with wavy sediment strata, sparse embedded pebbles plus larger irregular stones (lit top / shaded belly), hanging root tendrils and fine root hairs descending from the lip (faded where the rim is sand), root-shadow band, a ragged grass lip that turns into a deeper sand wash along the coast edges, and the path's sand column carried down the +z face. Unlit; sits below the ground/ocean planes. Module singleton, no controls.
- `src/Scenery.jsx` тАФ set dressing: a gabled cottage on the grass above the dune, stepping stones from its door to the path, and the beach-lounging vignette on the dry-sand band before the waterline тАФ placements in `HOUSE`/`CAMP`/`STONES` are keyed to the in-tile coastline in `coast.js`. The two figures have full limbs (lying figure: one straight leg + one bent knee-up with feet; sitting figure: feet flat on the sand and hands where the propped arms land), distinct skin tones (lying: `skin`, sitting: deeper `skinTan`/`skinTanShadow`), shoulder-joint spheres where the arms meet each torso, swimsuit straps on the lying figure's chest, and a simple face on the sitting figure. The sitting figure's arms/legs are `Limb`/`Segment` joint-point chains тАФ capsules stretched between shared joint coordinates, so segments always connect and pose edits mean moving joint points, not re-deriving capsule transforms; joint spheres are skin-colored (not shadow-toned) on both figures so elbows/knees don't read as mannequin balls. Face details: sitting figure тАФ dark oval eyes, blush dots, nose, mouth тАФ plus a low hair bun poking out behind the head under the hat brim, and a gentle idle `useFrame` in `Loungers` тАФ breathing chest swell/lift on both torsos, the sun hat rocking with the lying figure's breath, and the sitting figure's head (grouped with its hair so they pivot together) slowly scanning the horizon with a breath nod. Cottage build: (gable-end pentagon and chevron roof slab both built as `ExtrudeGeometry` along the ridge тАФ eaves/gable overhang/roof thickness come from the profile, no rotated-box math; ridge-cap cylinder along the peak, timber-framed gable (wall-height beam + raking beams) on the door end, arched extruded door + frame with knob, shuttered windows with mullion cross + sill and a flower box, round gable window, chimney with `useFrame`-animated smoke puffs), stepping stones toward the dirt path, and a beach-lounging vignette near the path exit (striped towel + lying figure with limbs and a straw sun hat over the face, sitting figure with knees-up legs / propped arms / hair cap, leaning parasol with an 8-wedge two-color striped canopy and finial). Plain Lambert materials lit by the rig in Rocks.jsx; no controls. Calls `stampSceneryMask` at module load so grass doesn't grow through footprints.
- `src/Birds.jsx` тАФ 5 low-poly gulls circling above the field (capsule body, sphere head, orange beak, forked shape-geometry tail, two-segment wings with a swept black-tipped outer panel; single white plumage, Lambert-lit like the scenery). Each follows a per-bird Lissajous path whose amplitudes are capped below `FIELD_HALF`, so flight is bounded to the tile by construction тАФ no steering/boids. `useFrame` samples the path at t/t+0.25/t+0.75 for position, heading (`lookAt`), and banked roll into turns; wings beat with a skewed (fast-upstroke) sine, the outer panel trailing the inner in phase, eased into intermittent glides by a smoothstep envelope biased by vertical velocity (beat uphill, glide downhill), with a downstroke body bob. The head (neck-pivot group) idly scans, looks into turns, and tilts down in glides; the tail rudder-yaws against turns, fans up in glide, and flicks with the beat. Each bird drags a blob contact shadow (radial-gradient `CanvasTexture` disc just above the ground, per-bird cloned basic material) that tracks x/z and grows/fades with altitude тАФ the ground/ocean shaders are unlit so real shadow maps can't reach them. Mirrored right wing: only the `scale.x=-1` node's own rotation.z negates тАФ nested rotations keep their sign. Module-singleton geometry/params, no controls.
- `src/rocks/Rocks.jsx` тАФ self-contained generative rock system: one `InstancedMesh` (module singleton, max 80) of a seeded sine-displaced, flat-shaded icosahedron with a 3-band `MeshToonMaterial` for the cel look, plus the ambient/directional lights that feed it (grass/ground materials ignore lights). A "rocks" leva folder drives count/size/variation/jaggedness/squash/spread plus a two-color gradient (color A/B, with a "gradient" slider scaling how much of the AтЖТB span is sampled) each rock samples at a random point for its tint; seed-gated like the grass тАФ sliders re-scatter the same layout, the "randomize" button rolls a new seed. Scatter rerolls positions that land past the in-tile coastline so rocks stay off the beach/water. `onBeforeCompile` injects the grass gust-sheen (same noise formula, shared uniform objects from `grass/material.js`) so gusts wash over rocks and grass together, plus a moss layer: world-space noise patches masked to up-facing surfaces, mixed into `diffuseColor` before the toon ramp so moss shares the cel bands. A "moss" sub-folder (amount/patch scale/color) writes transiently to module-level `mossUniforms` via `onChange` тАФ no re-render, no re-scatter.

## Conventions

- Geometry, param texture, and uniforms are module-level singletons тАФ the React Compiler lint forbids render-scope mutation, and there's only one `<Grass>`. Move them into the component if it ever mounts twice.
- `npm run lint` uses the React Compiler eslint rules тАФ strict about purity/mutation during render.
